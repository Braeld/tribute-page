Dr. Kenneth Kaunda

The first President of the Republic of Zambia

Queen Elizabeth II at Victoria Station greeting hatless President Kenneth Kaunda of Zambia, at the start of his four day State Visit.

Here's a time line of Dr. Kaunda's life:

  • 1924 - Born at Lubwa Mission in Chinsali, Northern Rhodesia (now Zambia).

  • 1941 - He attended the Munali Training Centre located in Lusaka.

  • 1943 - He began teaching at the Upper Primary School in Lubwa. He then became the Headmaster.

  • 1946 - He married Beatrice Kaweche Banda who bore eight children. They remained married until her death in 2012.

  • 1948 - He founded the farmers’ cooperative in a copper mining area where he also became a mine welfare officer after leaving the School in Lubwa.

  • 1949 - He served as the headmaster at Mufulira Upper School seeing that he couldn't stay away from teaching.

  • 1953 - Kaunda helped establish the African National Congress (ANC), the first key anti-colonial establishment organization in Northern Rhodesia. He served under ANC president Harry Nkumbula as the group’s secretary general.

  • 1958 - He became disenchanted with Nkumbula's policies and left the African National Congress to establish the Zambia African National Union. His party was banned in 1959 after upsurge in civil disobedience. This resulted in Kaunda spending nine months in jail.

  • 1960 - After his release, he became the president of the new United National Independence Party (UNIP). Two years later, he was elected to the Legislative Council. He then visited Martin Luther King Jr. in Atlanta, Georgia. Inspired by Martin Luther King, Jr., he went on to establish a civil disobedience campaign, known as the Cha-cha-cha campaign. The participants in this campaign engaged in arson and blocking major roadways.

  • 1964 - On 24th October, Zambia won its independence and Kaunda became the first president.

  • 1991 - Under pressure to create multiparty politics, he stepped down from the presidency.

  • 1997 - He retired from politics after being accused of being involved in a failed coup attempt.

  • 2021 - He currently resides in Lusaka and is celebrated as the father of the Republic of Zambia. Kaunda is 96 years old.
